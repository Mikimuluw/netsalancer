================================================================================
  DOCUMENT 2 — FXD/04/2026 DIRECTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

  NATIONAL BANK OF ETHIOPIA
  DIRECTIVE No. FXD/04/2026
  Issued: January 2026 | Effective: February 12, 2026

  PURPOSE
  -------
  Replaces all prior mandatory foreign currency surrender requirements.
  Grants exporters full retention rights over earned foreign exchange.

  KEY ARTICLES
  ------------

  ARTICLE 1 — SCOPE
  Applies to all persons and entities earning foreign exchange through
  the export of goods or services from Ethiopia.

  ARTICLE 2 — RETENTION RIGHTS
  Exporters may retain 100% of foreign exchange earnings in a
  designated foreign currency retention account at any licensed
  Ethiopian commercial bank.

  ARTICLE 3 — PERMITTED USE
  Retained foreign currency may be used for:
    - Import payments
    - International service payments
    - Debt servicing
    - Transfer abroad (within regulatory limits)
    - Conversion to ETB at prevailing market rates

  ARTICLE 4 — ACCOUNT REQUIREMENTS
  The retention account must be:
    - Held at a licensed Ethiopian commercial bank
    - Denominated in the original currency of receipt
    - Subject to standard KYC/AML requirements

  WHAT THIS MEANS FOR FREELANCERS
  --------------------------------
  1. You keep 100% of your USD/EUR/GBP earnings
  2. No mandatory conversion to ETB
  3. Legal, transparent income in your own name
  4. Bank statements that prove income for visas and loans
  5. No agent fees, no splitting, no informal channels

  Source: National Bank of Ethiopia
  Full text: https://nbe.gov.et/mandates/directives/

  DOCUMENT 3 — DOCUMENT CHECKLIST
================================================================================

  Use this checklist before visiting your bank. All items required
  unless marked (optional).

  IDENTITY
  [ ] Valid Ethiopian passport OR national ID (Kebele ID)
  [ ] Two passport-size photographs
  [ ] Proof of address (utility bill, lease agreement, or Kebele letter)

  TAX
  [ ] Tax Identification Number (TIN) certificate
      Get yours at: your local tax office or online at eservices.erca.gov.et

  BUSINESS DOCUMENTATION
  [ ] Business license (if registered) OR
  [ ] Freelance work permit OR
  [ ] Letter from local trade office confirming service-based work
  [ ] (optional) Professional certifications relevant to your field

  PROOF OF INTERNATIONAL INCOME
  [ ] Contracts or agreements with international clients (minimum 1)
  [ ] Invoices issued to international clients (minimum 3 recent)
  [ ] Payment receipts or bank transfer confirmations showing
      foreign currency received
  [ ] (optional) Platform earnings screenshots (Upwork, Fiverr, etc.)
  [ ] (optional) PayPal/Payoneer/Wise transaction history

  APPLICATION MATERIALS
  [ ] Completed cover letter (Document 1 in this package)
  [ ] Bank's own account opening application form
      (available at the branch)

  NOTES
  -----
  - Bring originals AND photocopies of all documents
  - Some banks may request additional verification
  - Processing typically takes 1-3 business days
  - You do NOT need a minimum deposit to open the account
  - Ask specifically for a "Foreign Currency Retention Account
    under FXD/04/2026"

  WEEK 1 — PREPARE
  +--------+------------------------------------------------------+

  | Day 8  | Submit application + all documents                    |

  WEEK 2-3 — ACTIVATE
  +--------+------------------------------------------------------+
  | Day 11 | Receive account confirmation                          |
  | Day 11 | Get account number and SWIFT/BIC code                 |
  | Day 12 | Update client payment details with new bank info      |
  | Day 14 | First international payment received                  |
  +--------+------------------------------------------------------+

  AFTER ACTIVATION
  +--------+------------------------------------------------------+
  | Month 1| Begin building bank statement history                 |
  | Month 3| UK/Germany visa applications become viable            |
  | Month 6| USA/Canada visa applications become viable            |
  +--------+------------------------------------------------------+

  TIPS
  ----
  - Visit during morning hours (branches are less crowded)
  - Ask for the "Foreign Exchange" or "International Banking" desk
  - Reference FXD/04/2026 by name — staff should recognize it
  - If a branch is unfamiliar with the directive, try a main branch
    or a different bank
  - Every month you delay = one month added to your visa timeline
